## Project Overview
This project analyzes marketing campaign data to evaluate which channels generate the highest ROI and conversion rates.

## Data Preparation
Before analysis, the dataset was prepared to enable more meaningful insights.
- A Duration_Group column was created from Duration_Days to categorize campaign durations into time ranges (0–15, 16–30, 31–45, and 46–60 days).
-  Additional calculated fields were created to support performance analysis, including:
- Month (derived from the campaign date for time-based analysis)
- CTR (Click-Through Rate) to measure engagement effectiveness
- CPC (Cost Per Click) to evaluate advertising efficiency
- Total Revenue to measure overall campaign financial performance.

These transformations enabled the creation of meaningful KPIs and visual insights for campaign performance evaluation.
 
## Project Structure
The Excel file contains:
- Raw Data → Original dataset
- Cleaned Data → Data after cleaning and formatting
- Pivot Tables → Analysis of clicks, conversions, CTR
- Dashboard → Visual summary of KPIs

## Observation
- Overall Marketing Performance (KPIs)
The dashboard shows that the marketing campaigns generated 109.95M total clicks from 1.10B impressions, resulting in a Click-Through Rate (CTR) of 28.08%. The campaigns generated a total revenue of $15.02B from $2.50B in acquisition costs, producing approximately 16,000 conversions.
This indicates that the marketing campaigns are achieving strong audience reach and engagement, with a relatively high CTR compared to typical industry benchmarks. However, the high acquisition cost suggests that campaign efficiency should still be monitored to maintain profitability.

- Innovate Industries and TechCorp show the strongest campaign performance with higher engagement levels, while NexGen Systems records comparatively lower results, suggesting variations in campaign effectiveness across companies.

- The 18–24 age group records the highest engagement, followed by women aged 25–34. Engagement declines among women aged 35–44, indicating stronger campaign responsiveness among younger audiences.
  
- Tech Enthusiasts and Foodies represent the most responsive customer segments, while Fashionistas show lower engagement, suggesting varying interest levels across segments.
  
- Campaigns running 16–30 days perform the best, while shorter campaigns show lower engagement. Performance also slightly declines for campaigns lasting over 45 days, indicating possible audience fatigue.
  
- All channels perform relatively well, but Email and Website channels generate the highest revenue and ROI, while social media channels contribute significantly to impressions and clicks.
  
## Recommendation
- Optimize Campaign Duration
Marketing campaigns should be strategically designed to run between 16 and 30 days, as this duration shows the highest engagement and performance levels. Campaigns shorter than this period may not generate sufficient exposure, while longer campaigns may lead to audience fatigue.

- Focus Marketing Efforts on High-Performing Segments
Given the strong engagement from Tech Enthusiasts and Foodies, marketing resources should prioritize these segments through targeted campaigns and personalized messaging. At the same time, strategies should be developed to better engage underperforming segments such as Fashionistas.

- Prioritize High-ROI Marketing Channels
Channels such as Email and Website marketing deliver the highest return on investment, making them valuable for conversion-focused campaigns. These channels should be prioritized for performance-driven marketing initiatives, while social media platforms can continue to be used for brand awareness and audience reach.

- Strengthen Targeting Toward Younger Audiences
Since the 18–24 and 25–34 age groups demonstrate the highest engagement, marketing campaigns should continue focusing on digital channels and content formats that resonate with these demographics. However, additional experimentation may be necessary to improve engagement among older audience groups.

- Improve Underperforming Company Campaign Strategies
Companies with lower campaign engagement, such as NexGen Systems, should review their campaign strategies, including audience targeting, creative content, and channel selection, to align more closely with successful performers like Innovate Industries and TechCorp.
